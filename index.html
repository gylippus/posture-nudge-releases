The weight of looking down

Your neck is carrying more than you think.

Mechanical neck pain is the quiet epidemic of desk-and-phone life — roughly half of us feel it in a given year, and screens are only tilting our heads further forward.

The damage isn't one bad moment. It's sustained load — hours held in the same slumped position. And the fix isn't perfect posture you can't hold. It's movement: small, frequent changes. That's the entire idea behind Posture Nudge.

The reset · how to fight back

What good looks like.

  1. 1
    Stack your head over your shoulders. Draw it straight back until your ears line up above your shoulders — not poked forward toward the screen.
  2. 2
    Level your chin. A gentle tuck, gaze straight ahead — not tipped up, not buried down.
  3. 3
    Let your shoulders drop. Roll them back and down, and open your chest.
  4. 4
    Bring the screen to eye level. The top of your display near eye height, so you look straight ahead instead of down.

And, when you can: stand up and move a little, and rest your eyes on something about 20 feet away for 20 seconds. No single position is right for long — light, frequent movement is what protects the neck over a day.
